EDPA

EDPA is a platform used by technical and non-technical employees together helping companies manage their big data. As the only designer in my team, I redesigned its information architecture, user flows, and 40+ interfaces with several engineers, and managers.

Before Starting

My knowledge in SQL and statistics made it easier to understand this product. However, I still had to learn a lot.

The most important 3 concepts in EDPA are category, term and data set. A category contains 0 or more terms, which can also be grouped by data sets.

Users

Same product, different goals

Non-technical and technical employees need to work on EDPA together, although they have different needs and goals. Non-tech people tend to only search for the data, while tech people need to manipulate the datasets and do CRUD.

Insight

EDPA is a data asset platform allowing non-technical and technical employees to collaborate together on big data in a digital era.

It's also a platform to help companies digitize and integrate their data asset on the cloud.

Information Architecture

My design focused on the Data Asset Management feature. The 3 major needs for users are: Check and manipulate the Asset Index (non-technical); Manage the Data Set (technical); Check Data Quality (both).

Since they are not closely related, I decided to put them in a hamburger navigation as three major tabs in Data Asset Management. When Charles is managing the dataset, he can also manage the data in the category-term hierarchy (since he has a higher authority, as shown by the small arrow connecting two hierarchies). However, it's rarer for Jack to use the dataset-term hierarchy.

Asset Index

Asset Index for non-technical employees

The tree view clearly shows the hierarchy of categories and terms, allowing users to find a category/term manually. There's also a global search area to help them search by keywords or status. Because of the limited space, I designed an Event Button to show notifications when some data update, or when there's a quality alert.

Dataset Management

Datasets for engineers

It took me a while to persuade engineers in my team to implement the Card-Based Design, by Competitor Analysis and A/B Testing. From my opinion, when there's only a few of the elements and we don't have to sort them, Cards are better than tables. Different datasets are differentiated by colors, which is clear and makes the design style more vivid.

Detailed Dataset

Turning on / off the automatic update is important. Therefore, the according button was designed blue and other buttons are icons from IBM design language and are greyscale. To be consistent, I also used card-based design on this page.

Pop-up Window

In order to help users better focus on what they should do, I used background blur and a wider color band for the pop-up window.

What did I learn from this project?

- Only when we understand the technical and business part of the product can we use design to drive it.
- Product and Design Thinking, and the design framework are important even for a time-demanding project.
- It's important to learn how to communicate with and persuade others without authority.
