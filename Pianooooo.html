Pianooooo

Service & UX Design / Independent Work

Project Brief

Pianooooo is a service design that have different functions and can be applied in different areas. Users can play the piano by scanning the QR code. Those who cannot play the piano can also try to play it like a game. Others can buy food, drink and related souvenirs as well as taking a rest at Pianooooo. After playing the piano, users who are interested in Pianooooo can purchase online courses and piano tickets via the App.

Pianooooo was realized by others several months after I finished my design, though in a relatively simple and primitive form(users can only scan the QR code to play the piano). Though disappointing, it proved the possibility and lucrativeness of such a business model, as well as my insight in the realm of business.

Bacground: In a hurry

Many people are suffering from anxiety and becoming workaholics nowadays, owning to their extremely fast lifestyle. As is reported, 21.5% of the people are suffering from anxiety and 2.5% are suffering from severe anxiety. For them, there is no time to take a break and rethink the meaning of life. Those under such intense pressure may end up never returning to normal life.

Play me, I'm yours

Touring internationally since 2008, Play Me, I'm Yours is an artwork by British artist Luke Jerram. Reaching over 10 million people worldwide, more than 1,700 street pianos have already been installed in over 55 cities across the globe, from London to New York, bearing the simple instruction Play Me, I'm Yours. (source:http://www.streetpianos.com)
Street pianos also attracted some famous pianists, such as Valentina Lisitsa and Lang Lang.

More than a piano

Those pianos are often painted in imaginative colors, which facilitates their aesthetic, as well as making them more noticeable. They should be also considered as attractions, instead of merely pianos. Attracted by the elegant music, people passing by would stop and stay there for a moment, which would not take too much time but instead give them a chance to calm down and escape from the hurry life.

SWOT Analysis: How Can We Improve Them?

Though the public piano is primarily an artwork currently, it has its potential to become a brand-new business model.

Field Trip & Interview: Where?

Public pianos are suitable at nearly any place, especially in hospitals, stations, schools, cafes and shopping centers where many people spend their considerable time waiting or doing other things.

I went to several representing places and interviewed some pedestrians, most of whom felt good about those pianos.

Public pianos also have different functions in diverse places. They can facilitate the recovery of patients, or mollify the people who are waiting for their flights. Most importantly, they can bring happiness to everyone around.

Usually these facilities are located in city centers where people lead a fast lifestyle and live under intense pressure. I hope to create a way to extend the time when people are staying near the public pianos.

Detailed Interview: Who?

I have interviewed several people about their attitude towards street pianos. Overall, almost all of them embrace this new type of invention, which brings them the chance to appreciate the beauty of music. Those who can play the piano tend to "show off", while others are willing to watch for a while.

App Design

Information Architecture

The major function of the App is to reserve/play a piano. Users can check the nearby pianos on the homepage and reserve one. In other cases, they can go to a nearby piano and scan the QR code to play it. Meanwhile, users can buy food, drink, souvenirs, online piano courses and piano tickets via the App. They can also recharge their wallets and view their purchases.

User Interface

Users can find a nearby piano at homepage and scan the QR code to play it.

 

They can buy piano tickets and online courses at a discount if they are interested in piano playing.

 

Users can also deposit their money in the "Wallet" and therefore buy food, drink and souvenirs.
